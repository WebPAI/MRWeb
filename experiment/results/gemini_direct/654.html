10 Disadvantages Of Being A Bookworm And How To "Fix" Them

By Lacey Kupfer Wulf | January 7, 2017 | Book Clubs, Book Lists, Book Lovers

Filed Under: Bookshelf Porn, Ebooks, Human Interest
Facebook Twitter Pinterest

As wonderful as reading is, there are pitfalls to being a literate.

Never fear, I’m not saying put down your books! I’m only observing some instances where reading can be a “Thing.” The downside to bookishness, and how to turn those inevitable results of constantly reading into something you can address, prevent, or even use to your advantage (gasp!).

1. BOOKS CAN LOOK MESSY

When you own so many books, they can take over every nook and cranny. Although Dr. Seuss would be delighted about it, it can be incredibly uncomfortable to get lost in your own literature. To prevent some of the clutter and “cluttered” with books and possibly even yourself, try neatly stacking. In the picture above, being organized is on the show. However, you still need to set aside some major unit for books organized.

Source: Decorator’s Notebook

Solution: There are thousands of book organization tips and tricks. If you own many, many copies of the in-vogue books, try to limit your book space by square footage and clean up on top of one layer of bookmarked pages in book. Your shelves have a lot of these.

2. READING CAN BE LONELY

Sometimes we bookworms prefer the company of paper, ink, and our imaginations over the company of real people. And that’s okay some of the time. But for those times when you crave human interaction more than people, it can be difficult to make friends with people who share your particular reading interests.

Source: Apple Shine NYC

Solution: Find others who share your affinity for reading by joining a reading group, book clubs, friends with your local librarians who love books and are usually really cool, or even down the book page to your barista and talk about people who are also interested in books. You do share interests, right? Well, if you don’t, you can try developing some.
