7 Things College Students Should Buy at Thrift Stores

Nick · August 10, 2017

It's that time of year when college students are thinking about or getting ready for their first year of college life. Living on campus or off campus and staying within your budget can be challenging. I was lucky and live only a few miles from the university. I was able to furnish their apartments, small flats, or dorms. Being a college student, I had to pay cool resources to show them some of the large and thrift stores around the area that could help them save money to pay. This made for happy, and it allowed for other activities such as fun road trips.

No matter how big or small your budget maybe in college, every student can benefit from thrift stores. Here is my list of 7 Things College Students Should Buy at Thrift Stores that will help you save some cash.

1. T-Shirts

Shirts at thrift stores tend to be very cheap allowing money to be spent on additional items.

2. Pants and Jeans

What was said above about t-shirts also applies to pants and jeans. That chemistry lab class, while almost always safe, does have the potential to stain or trash your clothes. Keep those thrift store clothes cheap $5-$9 or grab that nice pair of Levi jeans to wear for when you go out on the town and wear those dress pants to class.

Pants & Jeans can be purchased at thrift stores for a small fraction of retail costs.

3. Accessories

Since you are buying t-shirts and pants at the thrift store you should swing over to the accessories section and do a little shopping. Belts, ties, scarves, bags, purses, and vintage jewelry are always in stock and often overlooked. I have found more high dollar accessories at thrift stores for a fraction of what one would pay in retail stores. For men, a mid range class leather belt can easily run $50. At a thrift store that belt might be only $5 and in near new condition.

4. Books

Yeah, I know, we all have our phones and other tech gadgets. However, Books are smart. Books at thrift stores are cheap resources for textbooks and reference materials.

5. Dishware & Cookware

Dishware can be bought at discount retailers at cheap prices. Amazon runs great deals all the time. Going this route you are guaranteed new items that all match. If these routes or vintage looks, thrift stores are the way to go.

Low-cost cookware and bakeware such as these Pyrex dishes can be found at thrift stores.

6. Small Appliances

Microwaves, George Foreman grills, coffee makers, toaster ovens, blenders, slow cookers, and table top grills can all be found within in the first few dorm rooms on any floor in any of the dorms. Appliances 7 Things College Students Should Buy at Thrift Stores

Small kitchen appliances can be found at thrift stores for prices that are 50% to 90% less than retail.

7. Furniture

So you have a new place to live. You have your small appliances. But what about a table and chairs? Furniture 7 Things College Students Should Buy at Thrift Stores

Chairs are just one of many pieces of furniture one can find for reasonable prices at thrift stores.

Let the Savings Begin

As the start of the college year approaches it is never too early or too late to start hitting up the thrift stores to save some cash.

Remember to always thoroughly check items before purchase for any damage or functionality or safety.
