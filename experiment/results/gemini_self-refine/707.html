Public Libraries Censoring Bloggers.

← Lockerbie, Labour and Realpolitik Too stupid for sex? →

by Anna Raccoon on February 3, 2011

Censorship of the press is considered a heinous crime but even by those political parties who would cheerfully see the UK emulate North Korea in all manner of things.

Can you imagine the outcry if Oxfordshire County Council announced that you could no longer read the Guardian in their public libraries because they disliked an article published therein? Would twitter be similarly outraged, or questions raised in parliament?

Yet just this week a similar fate has befallen two bloggers and the silence is deafening – even from within the blogosphere.

 

The blog ‘Nutsville’ run by Tory party Westminster Council candidate Peter Large. Quote apart from the usual areas of debate for political activists, circulars to the web host alleging them to be racist, etc – the council have blocked the entire site. Being a council which presumes to provide public libraries, The FOI request that has been sent to the council by the 15th February to produce the notice – and proof of a material breach by violation of The Human Rights Act 1998, Article 10, Freedom of Expression – must be answered by the 25th and may be the start of putting other state (or in particular) council censorship in the spotlight.

You may want to read the full story on Martin Brookes, who runs one

 

Up in Scotland, a similar fate has befallen Caedmon’s
Cat whose operator is also active in reporting local Council. He too, we are told, has his site completely blocked in public libraries.

 

If a site contained libellous or defamatory material, then legal action is perfectly acceptable by those that it libels – and bloggers should have to comply with the law. However, local councils blocking entire sites which they object to from being read by the general public is outrageous, and any such defamation has been shown to occur in articles about all manner of people.

 

Why is this considered acceptable against bloggers but would be a matter of outrage against the main stream media?

 

Is anyone else aware of other instances of bloggers being censored in this way?

 

Do any of you have the time to drop a FOI into your local council’s IT department to see how wide spread this practice is, and which blogs are involved so that we can get a picture of what is happening country wide?

 

We are owed an list as to what the idiot, politically correct army of local councils, that have been given the right to decide what we read.
