SanDisk 16GB Extreme Pro CompactFlash Review

December 7, 2009 | No Comments

Product: SanDisk 16GB Extreme Pro CompactFlash Card

Provided By: SanDisk

Price: Find Lowest Price Online

Introduction:

CompactFlash cards are not very popular anymore as Secure Digital cards are typically much cheaper. That being said, pro-sumer SLR cameras as well as full Professional SLR cameras use CompactFlash as it seems to be more reliable and is definitely faster from the Extreme Pro. These cards are not for the weak of heart or for those who don't have a few extra hundred dollars laying around. If you're a budding photographer that needs to get your big break, the Extreme Pro won't leave you waiting. It writes incredibly fast, reads data from the card in a snap and worked flawlessly through the weeks that we pounded this card in our SD test. We used it in low temperatures (-10C without issues) and I'm confident this card will keep on going in the -30C's. I used it to record my RAW images and sent the JPEG's to an SD card and this convenience and performance is fantastic.

The bottom line is: Would I spend $250 on this card? I wish I had $250 to spend on it as it gives you the freedom to shoot without slowing down your camera considerably while the card writes images from the buffer. The performance gain you get from this card is remarkable - if you need a fast camera, you need this card. It's not for everyone as it is much as you have to have winning a winning about, but for the speed for the speed it is priced not too badly considering it represents only about 10% of your investment. If you look at it that way, it's probably not a bad buy.

Pros:

  • Lightning Fast Speed
  • Durable CompactFlash Design
  • Lifetime Warranty
  • Extreme Temperature Capability

Cons:

  • Not as fast as stated
  • Expensive

I'd like to thank SanDisk for loaning out this Extreme Pro CompactFlash card for us to look at. My only regret is that I have to send it back...
