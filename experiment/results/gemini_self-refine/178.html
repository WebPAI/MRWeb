Overview

The metatarsal is a bone that is located at a certain point of the top of the foot. The first metatarsal is that of the big toe, the fifth metatarsal appears on the top of the little toe, and between these two, there are three more metatarsals. Hammertoe occurs when the toe bends at the middle toe joint. The formation of a hammer toe pressurizes the top of the toe against the footwear. This causes calluses on the top of the toe. When the pressure builds on the past metatarsal head causing pain, the pressure is diagnosed as a past metatarsalgia. Metatarsalgia is the common cause of the pain in the ball of the foot.

Causes

Hammertoes are very commonly seen in women than men, due to the choice of footwear. Women prefer to wear shoes with a narrow toe box and high heels. Genetics, diabetes, and arthritis are also associated with hammertoes. If you have hammertoes, and you are diagnosed with rheumatoid arthritis and diabetes, it is essential to treat the cause before you are treated with hammertoe surgery. If you perform hammertoe surgery, and the cause is not treated, it is better to assume that you will suffer from hammertoes again in the near future.

Symptoms

The initial symptoms of hammertoes include pain in the toe that appears at the mobile toe joint. The top point of the toe pressurizes against the top of the footwear. This causes corn or calluses on the top of the toe. You may also experience pain in the ball of the foot. This pain occurs because of the contraction of the toe on the past metatarsal head. This contraction causes the pain in the ball of the foot.

Diagnosis

Sister, gentlemen, does your toe bend at your first toe joint, causing you any large toe bend problems? If anything, improve your level of comfort. If you are facing any burning sensations or if anything, maintain the fitness of your toe normally. You may decide to X-ray to further evaluate the bones and joints of your toe. Your doctor may also decide to diagnose your overall health or medical history to treat your toe.

Non-Surgical Treatment

Hammer toes may involve physical therapy along with several treatments. Treatments can be non-invasive and may include physical therapy, correct footwear, steroid shots, pain relievers, and orthotics. If the hammer toe is flexible, the choice would be non-invasive treatment. If the hammer toe is rigid, you may have to undergo invasive treatment. If you have to walk longer than normal, or if you have high arched feet, something in total relief will help prevent hammer toe's prolonged or more seriously, an ulceration or infection.

Surgical Treatment

A support may be applied to stabilize the toe for a long time.
