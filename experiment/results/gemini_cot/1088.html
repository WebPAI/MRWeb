Summer Soirée 2013 – A VIP Event in Support of SummerWorks Theatre Performance Festival

Sari Colt • June 28, 2013

Toronto’s historic Campbell House was the site for pre-SummerWorks Summer Soirée 2013 Launch Party to raise awareness for the 23rd annual SummerWorks Theatre Performance Festival. Guests that prefer wine and Miller beer drank along with hors d’oeuvres like smoked Gouda grilled cheese sandwiches from Lakeview Restaurant and Devil on Horseback dates wrapped from Samuel J Moore.

The various rooms in the Campbell House housed different theatre companies who performed teases from their upcoming SummerWorks shows or created one-of-a-kind pieces for the Soirée. Magician Joe Culpepper (Stone and Sky) entertained delighted guests with his magic card trick.

Joël Lavoie (Sale of Ouroboros) showed guests how to make their own dreamcatchers, which allows good dreams to be woven through the centre and nightmares to be caught in the web in the Web of Four Words piece. SummerWorks Artist-in-Residence and host and CBC Radio’s Definitely Not the Opera’s Sook-Yin Lee got strangers to call Adam Litovitz, Max Chandler, Stephanie Southmayd and Alice Lundy to the crowd to get them to join in creating a poem using only four words at a time. The witty poems were then sold for $4.00 or auctioned off to the highest bidder, much to the amusement of the crowd.

One highlight was the comedic, excerpt from Paradise Lost which follows the 20th year journey of the Starship Discovery to colonize a new uninhabited planet.

Bluemouth Inc. a game of strip poker where participants had to reveal a truth if mocked from their laws. And, some clothes did come off!

After the VIP event, doors were opened to the general public for the Launch Party with more treats, wines, live performances and music.

The festival runs from August 8-18 and a complete schedule of performances can be found at http://summerworks.ca
