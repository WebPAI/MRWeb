Summer Soirée 2013 – A VIP Event in Support of SummerWorks Theatre Performance Festival

By Sari Cott • June 26, 2013 • Arts & Culture, Entertainment & Media, Events, Festivals • 1 Comment

Toronto’s historic Campbell House was the site for SummerWorks Summer Soirée and Launch Party to raise funds and awareness for the 22nd annual SummerWorks Theatre Performance Festival. Guests that braved the torrential rain were greeted with tasty hors d'oeuvres like smoked gouda grilled cheese sandwiches from Lakeview Restaurant and Devil on Horseback bacon wrapped dates from Samuel J Moore.

The various rooms in the Campbell House housed different theatre companies who performed teases from their upcoming SummerWorks shows or created one-of-a-kind pieces just for the Soirée. Magician Joan Culpepper, Steve and Tali Rakowieck-Aronovitch delighted guests with their card magic trick.

Joël Lavoie (Sale of Words) showed guests how to make their own dreamcatchers, which allows good dreams to be caught in the web in the centre piece. Four Summerworks artists-in-residence and hosts of CBC Radio’s Definitely Not the Opera, Sook-Yin Lee and Adam Litovitz, Max Chandler, Stephanie Sutherland and Alice Ripley amused the crowd as they in turn creating a poem using only four words at a time. The witty poems were then sold for $4.00 or auctioned off to the highest bidder, much to the encouragement of the crowd.

One highlight was the comedic excerpt from Paradise Lost which follows the 20th year journey of the Starship Discovery to colonize a new uninhabited planet.

Bluemouth, a stage game of strip poker where participants had to reveal a truth if knocked from their seats. And, some clothes did come off!

After the VIP event, doors were opened to the general public for the Launch Party with more treats, wines, live performances and music.

The festival runs from August 8 – 18 and a complete schedule of performances can be found at http://summerworks.ca
