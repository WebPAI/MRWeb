I'm wearing my headphones so I don't have to talk to you...

SEPTEMBER 25, 2008 — SHINICHI

Yesterday, I was at Twiggs in University Heights to unwind around 6ish p.m., when I saw some people with clipboards outside by the entrance. I didn't know what they were up, but I just didn't care to find out. They may have even been for, but the strategic ambush posture didn't go very well

As I did my Internetting and sipped my coffee, I contemplated simply running past them. I imagined being rude. I contemplated my exit strategy. I roped into making a donation right there on the spot. I simply excuse that I had to meet some friends to get away from her, but happy that I got out of her grip.

I settled on wearing my headphones and listening to my iPod. However, they weren't deterred as two of them tried to rush me when I walked in the door. I then put my hand up in the classic "talk to the hand" gesture and moved on. I called Twiggs afterwards to voice a complaint about this.

A part of me felt rude for quickly dismissing them like that, but then again, they placed themselves in a position where they had a captive audience.

The outdoor solicitor problem got so bad at the Trader Joe's in Hillcrest/Uptown area that they put up a sign telling customers that they weren't obligated to talk to these street hawkers. It's pretty bad when we need institutional blessings to say "No."

I just did not want to be accosted by someone I did not want to get to know. However, they followed a few feet out the door. However, I resent that creative audience.

Kyle in Here in San Diego, they hit shopping centers, restaurants, schools, festivals, and cafes. Not to mention those ones you mention in peace. I'd take a page out of damnaiton if you don't agree with them, whereas these "professional beggars" often play on liberal guilt.
