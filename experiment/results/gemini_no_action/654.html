Never fear, I’m not saying put down your obsessively read book! Some circumstances can be a reading “fix.” This share dares to address these pitfalls, and how to turn them into adorable quirks. 2. READING CAN BE LONELY

Sometimes we bookworms prefer the company of paper, ink, and our imaginations over the company of real people. And that’s okay some of the time. But for those times when you crave more people than people, it can be difficult to make more friends with people who share your particular reading interests.

Solution: Find others who share your affinity for reading by joining a reading group, book clubs, friends with your local librarian(s) who love books and are usually really nice), or put down the book page for a little bit and talk to people about what interests you. You do have interests, right? Well, if you don’t, you can try developing some.
